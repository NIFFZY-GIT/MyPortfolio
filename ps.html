Chapter 1 - Introduction to Professional Skills

What is Professional Skills?

Professional skills, also known as soft skills or employability skills, are essential non-technical, interpersonal abilities needed for workplace success. Unlike technical skills, which are industry-specific, professional skills are transferable and applicable across various roles and industries. Key examples include teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability, leadership, and communication. Workplace skills can be categorized into three types,


  • technical skills
  • soft skills
  • transferable skills

The lecture also covered the importance of values, beliefs, attitudes, and character in shaping our decisions and interactions.

Core Aspects of Personal Growth

  • Values: Core principles like honesty, respect, and responsibility guide us in making the right decisions, especially in tough situations.
  • Beliefs: Personal convictions, such as self-efficacy and optimism, shape how we view ourselves and the world, influenced by experiences and culture.
  • Attitudes: These determine our reactions to people or situations, whether positive or negative, and are shaped by our values and beliefs. Examples include tolerance, open-mindedness, and empathy.
  • Character: This is the alignment of our actions with our values, beliefs, and attitudes, reflected in traits like trustworthiness, fairness, and caring.

Chapter 8 - Mastering Dining Etiquette

Mastering Dining Etiquette for Formal Settings

Dining etiquette, or table manners, is an essential skill that helps us navigate formal and casual dining settings with ease and confidence. This session focused on the key aspects of dining behavior, from seating arrangements to cutlery use, making conversations, and leaving a positive impression. Here's a summary of the main points

Dining Etiquette Tips

  • Seating Arrangements: The host signals guests to their seats, while guests should wait for guidance. Maintain good posture while seated, and keep your elbows off the table. Silence your phone and place your bag on a protected, elevated spot.
  • Table Setting Basics: Plates are centered with napkins placed on top. Forks are on the left (salad and main course), knives and spoons on the right (soup spoon and main course knife), and dessert utensils at the top. Bread plates are on the left, glasses (water, champagne, wine) on the right.
  • Using the Napkin: Place it on your lap during the meal. If stepping away, place the napkin on your chair to signal your return. When leaving the table for good, place it neatly on the left side of the plate.
  • Dining Techniques: Start with bread and butter, then move to the salad course and main course, using the appropriate utensils. Learn and use either the American or European style of eating. After finishing, place your utensils in a 12:00 to 6:00 or 10:00 to 4:00 position on the plate.
  • Ending the Meal: Place your napkin on the left side of the plate, signaling that you’re done. Follow all outlined behaviors to leave a polished impression.

Understanding Project Proposals: A Key to Success

As software engineering undergraduates, preparing project proposals is essential for tackling real world problems in the industry. A project proposal, often read in 5-10 minutes by clients, must be concise, clear, and convincing. There are two types of proposals, external and internal.


External Proposals

  • Executive Summary: A brief overview of technical, managerial, and financial details to convince decision makers.
  • Management Section: Highlights the team's capabilities, past successes, and implementation schedule.
  • Financial Section: Provides a detailed cost breakdown.

Internal Proposals

Aimed at gaining approval within an organization, these proposals start with identifying the problem and proposing meaningful, feasible solutions. Key components include:

  • Introduction: Purpose, problem definition, significance, solution, and preview of sections.
  • Discussion: Covers the problem, solution, and context to persuade internal stakeholders.

Understanding Proposals and Evaluation Reports

  • Purpose of a Proposal: It’s a structured way to present ideas and solutions, aiming for quick client or stakeholder approval.
  • Types of Proposals: External proposals target clients, while internal proposals focus on organizational acceptance.
  • External Proposal Components: Executive summary, management details, and financial breakdown are essential sections.
  • Internal Proposal Essentials: Requires problem identification, feasible solutions, and a well structured introduction and discussion.
  • Project Evaluation Reports: A future skill tied to assessing and documenting the effectiveness of implemented projects, like the food festival.

Negotiation Strategies

  • Win Win Approach: Negotiations should aim for outcomes that benefit both parties, fostering collaboration and mutual respect.
  • Preparation is Key: Researching the other party’s goals and setting clear objectives are essential for effective negotiation.
  • BATNA Strategy: Always have a backup plan to mitigate risks and boost confidence during negotiations.
  • Importance of Communication: Active listening, open body language, and clear communication are crucial for successful negotiations.
  • Knowing the Walk Away Point: Understanding when to step back prevents conflicts and ensures you don’t compromise unnecessarily.
